The Beaver Tail with No Meat

As a kid, when I heard of beaver tail for the first time, I was both confused and excited. Isn't a beaver tail really hard (considering beavers use it as a hammer for building) and isn't the beaver the Canadian emblem (like they're fine eating it?), so when my dad told me we'd go to try some, I was hyped as all heck. Until, I got there and saw the skors and Oreo flavors. And got to taste a crunchy and sweet fried pastry.

Beaver tail tastes great, don't get me wrong. In fact, I want to eat one right now. But to this day, some part of me, is kinda peeved at the deception.

Rambling aside, here is the recipe.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 5 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 pinch white sugar
  • 1 cup warm milk (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • ⅓ cup white sugar
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 5 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 quart vegetable oil for frying
  • Cinnamon Sugar:
    • 2 cups white sugar
    • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon, or to taste

Steps

  1. Mix warm water, yeast, and a pinch of sugar; let sit until creamy (about 5 minutes).
  2. Grease a separate bowl.
  3. Stir warm milk, eggs, ⅓ cup oil, ⅓ cup sugar, salt, and vanilla into yeast mixture until sugar dissolves. Stir in 2 ½ cups flour until no dry spots.
  4. Knead in remaining flour until dough is no longer sticky. Knead until smooth and elastic (about 10 minutes).
  5. Form dough into a ball, place in greased bowl, cover, and let rise until doubled (about 1 hour).
  6. Punch down dough and knead a few times to reshape.
  7. Pinch off egg-sized pieces of dough and roll into ¼-inch thick ovals. Place on a dish towel and cover.
  8. Heat 4 inches of oil to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  9. Make cinnamon sugar: Combine sugar and cinnamon in a shallow dish.
  10. Carefully lower dough into hot oil in batches and fry until golden brown (1 to 2 minutes per side).
  11. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  12. Gently press warm fried dough into cinnamon sugar. Shake off excess.
